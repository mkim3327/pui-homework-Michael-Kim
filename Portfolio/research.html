Inorganic Chemistry Lab*
Dr. Isaac Garcia-Bosch
Jan 2022 - Present
My research focuses on Lytic Polysaccharide Monooxygenases (LPMOs), particularly their role in oxidase reactions and potential involvement in C-H hydroxylation through a Fenton-like mechanism. Using synthetic copper complexes, I investigated the interaction between copper and oxygen, suggesting LPMOs' ability to generate hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) for oxidase reactivity. Currently, I'm advancing this work by modifying ligand scaffolds to better understand copper/O2 species. This research contributes to our understanding of enzymatic catalysis and holds promise for developing innovative catalysts for industrial and environmental applications.
Polymer & Material Lab
Dr. Stefanie A. Sydlik
Jan 2023 - Dec 2023
This research focused on developing functionalized graphenic materials (FGMs) for bone regeneration. Graphite was converted into graphene oxide and modified with functional groups to tailor its properties. Characterization confirmed FGMs' mechanical similarity to bone. In vitro tests with mammalian cells showed FGMs' ability to support cell growth and osteogenic differentiation. Degradation studies demonstrated biocompatibility. These findings highlight FGMs' potential as biomimetic scaffolds for bone tissue engineering, offering promising prospects for regenerative medicine.
Data Interaction Group Lab
Dr. Adamn Perer
Dec 2023 - Present
As a pivotal member of a major project, I focus on creating a clinical decision-support (CDS) tool tailored for Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension (PAH) care. My tasks include reviewing documents, refining interview protocols, and conducting clinician interviews to understand PAH's clinical nuances. This hands-on approach provides crucial insights for aligning the CDS tool with real-world practice. Additionally, I collaborate with the team to analyze interview data, guiding the iterative design process. This multifaceted involvement not only deepens my understanding of PAH but also empowers me to contribute to innovative solutions that could improve clinical decision-making and patient outcomes in PAH management.
